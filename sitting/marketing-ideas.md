If the vibe is similar to Ritesh Rana films, lean heavily into:

chaotic energy,
stylish comedy,
quirky characters,
recurring jokes,
cool edit pacing,
irony-driven marketing,
faux-serious promotion for absurd situations.

The ideal outcome:

Instagram reels become shareable memes,
Twitter discussions start fan-casting the feature version,
people quote dialogues before release,
YouTube comments demand a full-length film.
THE CORE STRATEGY

You need to market this in 3 layers simultaneously:

# Layer 1 — Sell the Characters

People don’t share plots.
They share personalities.

Your characters must feel like:
- “I know a guy exactly like this”
- “This idiot is my friend”
- “This dialogue is iconic”

Every marketing asset should introduce:
- quirks
- speech patterns
- flaws
- bromance chemistry

# Layer 2 — Sell the Director’s Voice

The director must become part of the brand.

You want people saying: “This feels fresh.”

Not: “Nice short film.”

You’re marketing a future filmmaker, not just a project.

# Layer 3 — Manufacture “Cult Film Energy”

Cult films feel:
- inside-jokey
- stylish
- self-aware
- community-driven

You need:
- recurring memes
- callbacks
- fake lore
- character posters
- “fans” interacting with characters
- ironic seriousness

# THE CAMPAIGN STRUCTURE
## PHASE 1 — “WHAT THE HELL IS THIS?” (2–3 weeks before teaser)

Goal: Create curiosity without explaining everything.

Do NOT announce: “Our short film is coming.” That is low-energy.

Instead create:
- character incidents
- fake screenshots
- funny “real-life” moments
- chaotic clips

### CHARACTER-FIRST MARKETING

## 1. Fake WhatsApp Chats

#### Create designed screenshots:
- one character asking another for money,
- absurd plans,
- failed crimes,
- dumb motivational quotes,
- chaotic misunderstandings.

Post as carousel slides. These perform extremely well because:
- people read them fully,
- they are shareable,
- they feel native to Instagram.

**Example:**

Character A: “Bro one last plan. Life set.”

Character B: “You said this before selling my scooter.”


## 2. “Police Evidence” Posters

### Instead of normal character posters. Create:

- FIR-style posters,
- police mugshots,
- “missing person” posters,
- CCTV screenshots,
- fake newspaper cuttings.

This creates instant tone.
