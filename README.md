# Promote and Boost Short Film Reach as well as establish the presence of the shortfilm production house (makeamovieproductions)

### Mission Statement: Making a film needs nobody’s permission, it needs initiation. We took the initiative and started making something that we like and something we would like to see on the big screen. Sometimes we just need to start, just make a movie and learn on the go.

- Social media presence, tailored posts based on platforms (twitter,instagram,youtube)
- Twitter is to share updates about the making of the short film and once the short film is released, thank the involved technicians, etc
- Instagram to share making videos, the music composition process, the original OST track (find out how to upload on spotify/youtube), make a carousel post of the making of the short film (pre-production, production, post-production) documentation
- Youtube to post the short film, collect feedback from comments, find out the response of the audience and understand the short film performance based on analytics of the video, post the OST of the short-film separately; make a long form video explaining the BTS process (from the director)
- Keep making YT shorts/Insta reels that are engaging and explain the making of a shortfilm and the hurdles

___
This is a fantastic mission statement. It taps into the "democratization of filmmaking" movement, which resonates deeply with modern audiences who value authenticity over high-budget polish.

As a content creator and strategist, I’ve broken your plan down into foundational "Basics," "Innovative Hooks," and a sustainable "Monetization Strategy" that moves beyond simple one-off transfers.

## 1. The Strategy: Platform-Specific Content

### YouTube (The "Anchor" Platform)
- Basic Idea: The "Director’s Commentary" Track. Instead of just a BTS video, release a version of the film with a picture-in-picture of the director explaining why certain shots were made. It builds a personal connection.
- Innovative Idea: The "Modular" Edit. Create a YouTube Short that asks the audience to vote on an ending or a specific plot point for the next film. This turns viewers into "producers" and keeps them invested in the channel’s growth.
- Technical Tip: For the OST, use DistroKid or Tunecore to get your music on Spotify. It’s cheap, handles the royalties, and automatically creates "Art Tracks" on YouTube.

### Instagram (The "Vibe" & Community Platform)
- Basic Idea: Gear Breakdown Carousels. Show a high-quality shot, then swipe to show the "ugly" reality of the DIY lighting or camera rig used to get it. It reinforces your "no permission needed" mission.
- Innovative Idea: "The Sound of [Film Name]." Use Reels to showcase "Foley" (sound effects) creation. People love watching someone smash a watermelon to simulate a specific sound. It’s highly shareable and "ASMR-adjacent."

### Twitter/X (The "Industry" Platform)
- Basic Idea: The "Tech-Stack" Thread. Post a thread detailing the camera, lens, and software used. Tag the brands (Sony, Adobe, Blackmagic); they often retweet indie creators using their gear.
- Innovative Idea: "Daily Hurdles." Post one specific problem you faced that day (e.g., "The lead actor got a flat tire") and how you solved it. It builds a narrative of resilience.

## 2. Content Pillars (The "Initiative" Series)
- To establish makeamovieproductions, you need content that exists even when you aren't releasing a film.
- "The $0 Fix": A series showing how to achieve cinematic effects with household items (e.g., using a shower curtain as a light diffuser).
- "Script to Screen" Side-by-Sides: Post a split-screen video: the raw script on the left and the final shot on the right. It’s educational and visually satisfying.

## 3. Monetization: Moving Beyond Zelle
- While Zelle is great for friends, asking for it under a YouTube video can feel a bit "informal" and lacks "buyer protection," which might make strangers hesitant. To look like a professional Production House, consider these tiers:
- The "Tip Jar" (Low Friction)
- Buy Me a Coffee: This is the industry standard for creators. It’s a clean interface where people can "buy you a coffee" ($5) to support the next film. You can put this link in your YouTube description and Instagram Bio.
- YouTube Super Thanks: Once you hit the partnership requirements, enable this so people can tip you directly on the video.
- The "Inner Circle" (Recurring Income)

### Patreon / YouTube Memberships: Offer tiers for true fans:
- $3/mo: Early access to films.
- $10/mo: Access to the raw scripts and "making-of" documents.
- $25/mo: Their name in the "Special Thanks" credits of the next film.

### The "Product" (Digital Assets)
- Sell your OST: Use Bandcamp to allow people to "pay what they want" for the soundtrack.
- LUTs and Sound Packs: If you have a specific "look" or color grade people like, sell the "MakeAMovie Film Look" as a LUT pack for other aspiring filmmakers.
